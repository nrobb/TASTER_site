Guide for Participants

To help us design our game, we'd like your child / the person you care for to play a selection of games over a period of two weeks, and tell us what they think of them. The information on this page is for parents / legal guardians.

At the start of the two week period:

  1. You can see the full list of games, along with a description of each game and a video, here. Read about each game and watch the video. If there are any games that you don't want your child / the person you care for to play, then you don't have to consider those games any further.
  2. For each game that you are happy for your child / the person you care for to play, follow this process:
    • Load the game in your web browser / install the game on your tablet.
    • Present the game to your child / the person you care for, without explaining the game to them.
    • Observe your child / the person you care for using the game for a brief period (less than 5 minutes).
    • For each game, make a note of how well your child / the person you care for understands what they are supposed to do in the game (Doesn't understand at all / Understands a little / Understands well). You'll need this information later, when you complete the Initial Comprehension Form. You can show your child / the person you care for this image, to help them decide.
  3. When you have completed this process for all the games, complete the Initial Comprehension Form. You'll need to complete the form in one sitting, but if you've recorded your child's / the person you care for's progress throughout, the form should only take a minute or so to complete.
  4. At this point you can explain each game further, so that your child / the person you care for fully understands how to play the game.

During the two week period:

  1. Give your child / the person you care for free access to all the games that you are happy for them to play.
  2. At the end of each day, you and your child / the person you care for should complete the Daily Feedback Form.
  3. It is important that your child / the person you care for plays as many of the games as possible. Encourage them to try all the games, and not just their favourites.

After the two week period, a final form will become available for you and your child / the person you care for to complete.
